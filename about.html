About Me

I am a graduate student at the University of Michigan pursuing a Master of Science in Information with a concentration in Human-Computer Interaction. I graduated from Carleton College in 2021 with a major in History and a minor in Digital Arts & Humanities. I am passionate about creating people-first technology that is accessible, inclusive, and empathetic.


Digital Humanities


As a History major, one of the primary things I learned is that stories have power: how a story is told, what parts are emphasized, and for whom it is intended shape the communities around us. I believe that technology is a potent storytelling device: data visualizations craft narratives, websites host our stories, and creative programs like Photoshop bring our artistic visions to life. As a digital humanist, I aspire to tell stories through technology that amplify marginalized voices, connect communities, and bridge gaps between people, technology, and information.


Public Engagement and Community Service


When I was 18 years old, I convinced my mother to run for mayor of my hometown and dedicated my senior year to working on her campaign. This experience instilled in me a passion for community engagement, service, and a commitment to the social good that I have carried over to my professional career. I believe that technology is a powerful tool for social change and should be used to create a kinder, more compassionate world. To that end, I have worked with a number of community partners, including historical societies, local artists, and non-profits, to advocate for these values and support their work.


User Experience Research and Design


Every product tells a story. I aim to craft a narrative that is centered around the user and their experience, one that is relatable to and enjoyed by all kinds of people. Through this digital storytelling, I hope to invite joy, curiosity, and social connection—-to restore humanity to technology.
